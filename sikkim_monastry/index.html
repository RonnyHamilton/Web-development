Sikkim Monasteries

Sikkim is home to many significant monasteries (Gompas) that are centers of spiritual and cultural life, including the famous Rumtek Monastery, the seat of the Kagyü lineage of Tibetan Buddhism, and the ancient Pemayangtse Monastery. These monasteries, built in distinctive Tibetan architectural style with intricate murals, thangkas, and statues, are located in scenic areas and showcase the deep Buddhist heritage of the state. Sacred Texts

Ancient Manuscripts & Sacred Texts

Discover the invaluable collection of Buddhist manuscripts preserved within Sikkim's monasteries, each telling stories of wisdom passed down through generations.

📜

Kangyur Collection

The sacred collection of Buddha's direct teachings, containing over 108 volumes carefully preserved at Rumtek Monastery. Many texts were carried from Tibet during the great migration.

📚

Tengyur Manuscripts

Ancient commentaries and treatises by Indian and Tibetan masters, featuring detailed explanations of Buddhist philosophy, logic, medicine, and arts, housed in various Sikkim monasteries.

🗝️

Terma Treasures

Hidden spiritual treasures and prophetic texts discovered by tertons (treasure revealers) in sacred sites across Sikkim, including teachings attributed to Guru Rinpoche.

About Monastery360

Monastery360 is a student project aligned with SIH-2025 that aims to digitally preserve the rich heritage of Sikkim’s monasteries. The site is engineered to be risk-free: no third-party scripts, no trackers, and all heavy content is optional and opens externally (e.g., Street View, Google Maps).

Source images in this demo are placeholder art embedded as SVG for offline use. You can swap them with your own photos easily—see the developer notes in code.
