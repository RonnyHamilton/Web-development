Sacred Texts

Ancient Manuscripts & Sacred Texts

Discover the invaluable collection of Buddhist manuscripts preserved within Sikkim's monasteries, each telling stories of wisdom passed down through generations.

Kangyur Collection

The sacred collection of Buddha's direct teachings, containing over 108 volumes carefully preserved at Rumtek Monastery. Many texts were carried from Tibet during the great migration.

Tengyur Manuscripts

Ancient commentaries and treatises by Indian and Tibetan masters, featuring detailed explanations of Buddhist philosophy, logic, medicine, and arts, housed in various Sikkim monasteries.

Terma Treasures

Hidden spiritual treasures and prophetic texts discovered by tertons (treasure revealers) in sacred sites across Sikkim, including teachings attributed to Guru Rinpoche.

Ethnicity & Traditions

Diverse Cultures of Sikkim

Sikkimâ€™s heritage is shaped by a harmonious blend of communitiesâ€” primarily the Bhutia, Lepcha, and Nepali ethnic groupsâ€”each contributing unique traditions, festivals, attire, and art forms that enrich monastery life.

Lepcha Traditions

Known as the original inhabitants of Sikkim, the Lepchas practice deep reverence for nature. Their festivals like Tendong Lho Rum Faat celebrate mountain spirits, and their weaving crafts are renowned for natural dyes.

Bhutia Heritage

The Bhutias brought Tibetan Buddhist traditions to Sikkim. Distinctive attire like the Bakhu, colorful prayer flags, and festivals such as Losar (Tibetan New Year) highlight their cultural legacy tied closely to monasteries.

Nepali Influences

The Nepali community, the largest in Sikkim, contributes vibrant folk dances, festivals like Dasain and Tihar, and agricultural practices. Their traditions have blended with Sikkimâ€™s Buddhist culture over centuries.
